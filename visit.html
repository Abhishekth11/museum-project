Getting Here

By Car

The museum is located in the heart of downtown. Follow signs for the Cultural District.

GPS coordinates: 40.7128° N, 74.0060° W

Public Transportation

Take the Blue Line to Museum Station or the Red Line to Central Station (10-minute walk).

Bus routes 10, 15, and 22 stop directly in front of the museum.

Parking

The museum has a parking garage located beneath the building. Entrance is on Museum Street.

  • First hour: $5
  • Each additional hour: $3
  • Daily maximum: $20
  • Members receive a 50% discount on parking fees.

Accessibility

The National Museum of Art & Culture is committed to making our facilities, exhibitions, and programs accessible to all visitors.

Physical Access

  • The museum is fully wheelchair accessible.
  • Elevators provide access to all floors.
  • Accessible restrooms are available on every floor.
  • Wheelchairs are available free of charge at the Information Desk.

Hearing Accessibility

  • Assistive listening devices are available for all guided tours and public programs.
  • Sign language interpretation is available with two weeks' advance notice.
  • All videos in exhibitions include captions.

Visual Accessibility

  • Large-print exhibition guides are available at the Information Desk.
  • Tactile tours for visitors with visual impairments can be arranged with advance notice.
  • Service animals are welcome in all areas of the museum.

Sensory & Cognitive Accessibility

  • Sensory kits with noise-canceling headphones and fidget tools are available.
  • Quiet spaces are available throughout the museum for visitors who need a break.
  • Social narratives and visual schedules can be downloaded from our website.

For specific accessibility requests or questions, please contact our Accessibility Coordinator at access@nmac.org or call (123) 456-7890 ext. 123.

Group Visits

We welcome groups of 10 or more visitors. Group benefits include:

  • Discounted admission rates
  • Priority entry
  • Option to add a private guided tour
  • Reserved dining space (with advance notice)

Group visits must be scheduled at least two weeks in advance.

We offer specialized tours and programs for K-12 school groups, aligned with curriculum standards.

School group visits are free for public and charter schools within the city.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I take photographs in the museum?

Photography for personal use is permitted in most areas of the museum. Flash photography, tripods, and selfie sticks are not allowed. Some special exhibitions may prohibit photography.

Is there a coat check or bag storage?

Yes, we offer complimentary coat and bag check services in the main lobby. Large backpacks and luggage must be checked.

Are strollers allowed in the museum?

Yes, strollers are welcome in all public areas of the museum. Stroller parking is available in designated areas for certain special exhibitions.

Is Wi-Fi available?

Free Wi-Fi is available throughout the museum. Connect to the "NMAC-Guest" network.

Can I bring food or drinks into the galleries?

Food and beverages are not permitted in the galleries. Water in closed containers is allowed.

What items are prohibited?

Prohibited items include weapons, illegal substances, markers, pens (pencils are available at the Information Desk), and items larger than 16 x 16 inches.

Have more questions? Contact us or visit the Information Desk during your visit.
